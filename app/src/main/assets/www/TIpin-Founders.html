The university's founders, upon breaking ground on what would be Founder's Hall, placed a time capsule at the base, meant to be open in 100 years' time.




During a renovation in the 70s, the capsule was accidentally unearthed. It wasn't opened, but was lost and hasn't been reburied for its intended purpose.




Given how much of a forward-facing institution UTD is, what if a relic of the past, something tangible to tie down UTD's roots, hadn't be lost or modified?





Preservation might have a more prominent position at the university, and as a result, there would be more interest and critical attention given to the university's origins.
