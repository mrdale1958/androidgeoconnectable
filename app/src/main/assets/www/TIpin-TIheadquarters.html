UTD's very distant origin was in the initial discussions of Drs. Erik Jonsson, Eugene McDermott, and Lloyd Berkner after they founded Texas Instruments. They wanted a research facility. After honing the focus, the founders created the Graduate Research Center of the Southwest (GRCSW) in 1961, funded partially by government funds and TI shares.




Drs. Jonsson and Berkner were the key figures in the founding of the GRCSW, which gathered a great deal of interest from academics and military leaders, unsurprisingly.




After making discoveries for over eight years in space and geosciences, the GRCSW was integrated into the UT system in 1969 as the University of Texas at Dallas.br>
